Before sending DOT or KSM to any address, you should verify that the recipient is legitimate. Scammers frequently impersonate known projects, validators, and treasury accounts. Here's how to check.

## Step 1: Scan the Address on Wisesama

The fastest way to check any Polkadot or Kusama address is to scan it on [Wisesama](https://wisesama.com).

1. Go to [wisesama.com](https://wisesama.com)
2. Paste the wallet address into the search bar
3. Click "Scan Now"

Within seconds, you'll get a comprehensive risk assessment including the address's risk level, on-chain identity, transaction history, and whether it appears on any blacklists.

### What the Risk Levels Mean

- **Safe (Green)** - The address belongs to a verified, whitelisted entity. Still verify the details match what you expect.
- **Unknown (Gray)** - No data available. This doesn't mean it's safe. Proceed with caution.
- **Caution (Orange)** - Risk indicators detected. This could be community reports, suspicious transaction patterns, or similarity to known scam addresses.
- **Fraud (Red)** - Confirmed malicious. Do not send funds to this address.

## Step 2: Check On-Chain Identity

Polkadot has a built-in identity system on the People Chain. Legitimate projects and validators typically register and verify their identity on-chain.

When you scan an address on Wisesama, the identity section shows:

- **Display name** - The name registered on-chain
- **Verification status** - Whether a registrar has verified the identity (look for "Reasonable" or "Known Good" judgements)
- **Social links** - Twitter, website, GitHub, and other linked accounts
- **Timeline** - When the identity was first set and verified

An address with no on-chain identity isn't necessarily a scam, but verified addresses with registrar judgements are significantly more trustworthy.

## Step 3: Verify the Domain

If someone sent you a link alongside the address, check the domain too. Phishing sites often use domains that look almost identical to legitimate ones.

Wisesama scans domains against 70+ security engines via VirusTotal and checks for look-alike patterns against known ecosystem domains.

Common red flags:
- Domains with extra characters (e.g., `polkadot-claim.xyz` instead of `polkadot.network`)
- Recently registered domains
- Domains flagged by multiple security engines

## Step 4: Check Transaction History

Wisesama shows a transaction summary for scanned addresses:

- **Total transactions** - Very new addresses with zero history should be treated cautiously
- **Balance** - Scam addresses often have many small incoming transactions (victims) and rapid outflows
- **Last activity** - Dormant addresses that suddenly become active may indicate compromised keys

## When in Doubt, Don't Send

No legitimate project will pressure you to send funds immediately. If you're unsure about an address:

1. Scan it on Wisesama first
2. Verify the on-chain identity matches the claimed entity
3. Cross-reference with official project channels
4. Ask in community channels before transacting

If you've encountered a scam address, [report it on Wisesama](/report) to help protect others in the ecosystem.
