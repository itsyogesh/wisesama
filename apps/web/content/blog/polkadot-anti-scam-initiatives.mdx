While scams plague the entire crypto industry, Polkadot has taken a unique approach to protecting its community. It's the **first blockchain ecosystem with a dedicated and decentralized Anti-Scam Team** that has taken great strides in safeguarding users.

## The Anti-Scam Bounty Program

Polkadot incentivizes its community to fight scams through an innovative "anti-scam bounty" program. Community members can earn rewards for:

- Identifying and reporting scam websites
- Flagging fraudulent addresses
- Helping victims recover from scam attempts
- Contributing to scam prevention tools

## The Anti-Scam Team

The team serves as the first line of defense with two main tracks:

### Reactive Track
Responsible for investigating and responding to instances of scams in the Polkadot ecosystem, including:
- Assisting users who have lost funds
- Tracking down fraudulent actors
- Coordinating with exchanges to freeze stolen funds

### Preventive Track
Focuses on stopping scams before they happen through:
- Education and awareness campaigns
- Tool development (like wallet scam alerts)
- Community training programs

## Crypto Defenders Alliance

Web3 Foundation has joined the **Crypto Defenders Alliance (CDA)**, a consortium of exchanges and wallets working together to prevent money laundering. When a scam victim reports to CDA members, all associated addresses are blocklisted across participating platforms.

## Common Scams to Watch For

The Polkadot team warns about these prevalent scam tactics:

- **Fake admin messages** - Real Polkadot team members will never DM you first
- **Giveaway scams** - "Send us DOT, we'll send double back" is always a scam
- **Phishing sites** - Sites asking for your seed phrase to "sync" or "unlock" your account
- **Discount offers** - No one legitimately sells DOT at a discount

## Built-in Protection

Polkadot wallet extensions now recognize known scam sites and alert users before they can enter sensitive information. This browser-level protection has prevented countless phishing attempts.
